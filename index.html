Note WTI and Brent were under pressure in January, with WTI trading below $58/bbl, before a sharp inflection in late February/early March coinciding with the onset of Operation Epic Fury. The widening in the Brent–WTI spread is a notable signal: Brent trades at a premium when Middle East supply routes are threatened, since it prices seaborne crude. A widening spread is the market's real-time estimate of Strait of Hormuz disruption risk — and it is currently at elevated levels not seen since 2022.
