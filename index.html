Feb 28
Military Opening Strikes. The United States and Israel launch coordinated surprise airstrikes — designated Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel) — targeting military installations, air-defense networks, and command compounds across Iran.
Military Leadership Decapitation. Strikes destroy the Supreme Leader's compound in Tehran, killing Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei, Defense Minister Aziz Nasirzadeh, IRGC Ground Forces Commander Mohammad Pakpour, and several other senior officials.
Humanitarian School Strike. A strike hits a girls' elementary school adjacent to an IRGC facility in Minab, killing approximately 170–180 people, the majority children. The incident draws immediate international condemnation.
Military Operation True Promise IV. Iran retaliates with a mass barrage of ballistic missiles and drones targeting Israel, U.S. military bases, and allied Gulf states including the UAE, Bahrain, Kuwait, and Qatar.
Energy Hormuz Closure Announced. The IRGC announces the closure of the Strait of Hormuz to all maritime traffic — a chokepoint through which roughly 20% of global oil supply transits — triggering an immediate shock in energy markets.
Mar 1
Political Iranian Leadership Transition. State media officially confirms Khamenei's death. An Interim Leadership Council is established to govern pending designation of a new Supreme Leader.
Military Second Wave of Strikes. Israel launches a follow-on strike campaign. Iran fires missiles at Israel, the UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Jordan, and Saudi Arabia. A missile hits a public shelter in Beit Shemesh, Israel, killing nine civilians.
Maritime Tanker Attacks. Two oil tankers — Skylight and MKD Vyom — are struck by Iranian forces off the Omani coast, with casualties reported among crew members.
Diplomatic UK Authorizes Base Access. The United Kingdom grants the U.S. use of military facilities in Cyprus, Diego Garcia, and the British mainland for limited defensive purposes, including interception of Iranian missiles.
Mar 2
Energy Blockade Confirmed. A senior IRGC official formally confirms the Strait of Hormuz closure and threatens to set fire to any vessels attempting to transit.
Military Friendly Fire Incident. A Kuwaiti F/A-18 mistakenly shoots down three U.S. F-15 fighters during air-defense operations. Investigations are ongoing.
Military Lebanon Front Opens. Hezbollah commences rocket attacks on northern Israel in retaliation for Khamenei's death. The Lebanese government responds by officially banning Hezbollah's military activities — a largely symbolic measure.
Diplomatic U.S. Embassy Struck. The U.S. Embassy in Kuwait is hit by an Iranian missile and closed indefinitely. Non-essential staff are evacuated.
Mar 3
Military Strategic Facilities Destroyed. U.S.-Israeli strikes destroy the Supreme National Security Council headquarters, the Expediency Discernment Council building, and an underground nuclear-linked facility designated Taleghan 2 at the Parchin military complex.
Political Assembly of Experts Targeted. Israel bombs a preliminary session of Iran's 88-member Assembly of Experts — the constitutional body responsible for selecting the next Supreme Leader — in an apparent attempt to prevent succession.
Military Lebanon Invasion Authorized. Israel formally authorizes and initiates a ground incursion into southern Lebanon in response to sustained Hezbollah rocket fire.
Mar 4
Maritime IRIS Dena Sunk. The U.S. Navy submarine USS Charlotte torpedoes and sinks the Iranian Navy frigate IRIS Dena in the Indian Ocean — one of the most significant naval engagements in the conflict to date.
Military First F-35 Air-to-Air Kill. An Israeli F-35I Adir shoots down an Iranian Yak-130 over Tehran — marking the first time an F-35 has destroyed a manned enemy aircraft in combat.
Energy QatarEnergy Force Majeure. Following Iranian attacks on Qatari infrastructure, QatarEnergy declares force majeure on all LNG shipments, instantly removing approximately 20% of global LNG supply from the market.
Mar 5
Diplomatic Azerbaijan Targeted. Two Iranian drones strike Azerbaijan's Nakhchivan exclave, damaging an airport and injuring civilians. Azerbaijan withdraws its diplomats from Tehran in protest.
Energy Selective Strait Access. The IRGC announces that the Strait of Hormuz will remain closed to the U.S., Israel, and Western allies — but Chinese-flagged vessels will be permitted passage, signaling a geopolitical carve-out.
Mar 6
Military Air Superiority Established. The IDF destroys 16 Quds Force aircraft at Tehran's Mehrabad International Airport. U.S. CENTCOM states it has achieved air superiority over large swathes of Iranian territory.
Diplomatic Terror Plot Foiled. Azerbaijani law enforcement disrupts an Iranian-linked plot targeting the Israeli Embassy and a major oil pipeline in Baku, further straining Iran-Azerbaijan relations.
Mar 7
Political Civilian Government vs. IRGC Split. Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian publicly apologizes to Gulf neighbors and orders a halt to attacks against them. The IRGC defies the directive and continues striking Arab states — signaling a critical fracture in Iran's command structure.
Energy Oil Infrastructure Targeted. For the first time in the campaign, U.S.-Israeli forces extend airstrikes to directly hit Iranian oil production facilities and storage depots, further tightening global supply.
Mar 8
Political New Supreme Leader Designated. Iran's Assembly of Experts officially names Mojtaba Khamenei — son of the slain Ali Khamenei — as the new Supreme Leader of Iran.
Energy Oil Tops $100/bbl. Global crude prices breach $100 per barrel for the first time in four years as Middle Eastern output plummets and the Strait of Hormuz remains closed. Both Brent and WTI post the sharpest weekly gains in years.
Environmental Toxic Fallout Near Tehran. Israeli strikes on fuel storage depots near Tehran ignite massive fires, producing a "river of fire" and causing toxic acidic black rain across surrounding residential areas.
Mar 9
Humanitarian Cluster Munitions Used. Two Israeli civilians are killed in Yehud by submunitions from an Iranian missile equipped with a cluster-bomb warhead — the first confirmed use of cluster munitions against Israeli population centers.
Diplomatic NATO Airspace Incident. NATO integrated air-defense systems intercept a second Iranian ballistic missile that enters Turkish airspace, raising concerns about escalation involving Article 5 of the NATO charter.
Mar 10
Military Iranian Missile Posture Shifts. A senior IRGC commander announces Iran will fire only high-payload missiles (≥1,000 kg warheads). However, U.S. intelligence notes Iran's overall missile launch rate has fallen by approximately 90% since Day 1, indicating significant depletion of its arsenal.
Maritime Strait Mining Attempted. U.S. military sources report Iran has begun planting naval mines in the Strait of Hormuz. U.S. Navy forces destroy 16 Iranian minelaying vessels in response.
Mar 11
Diplomatic UN Security Council Resolution. The UN Security Council passes a resolution condemning Iran's attacks on Gulf states and classifying them as violations of international law. Iran rejects the resolution.
Maritime Iraq Terminal Operations Suspended. Iranian drone-boats attack two oil tankers — Safesea Vishnu and Zefyros — during a transfer near Basra, Iraq, killing one crew member and forcing Iraq to suspend all oil terminal operations.
Diplomatic Swiss Embassy Evacuated. Switzerland — which serves as the primary U.S.-Iran diplomatic backchannel — temporarily closes its Tehran embassy and evacuates staff, effectively severing the last formal diplomatic conduit.
Mar 12
Military Bunker-Buster Strikes on Parchin. U.S. and Israeli forces deploy GBU-57 Massive Ordnance Penetrator bombs against the Taleghan 2 site at Iran's Parchin military complex — a facility previously linked to high-explosives testing associated with nuclear device development.
Military Hezbollah Escalation — Lebanon Front. Hezbollah claims 27 separate attacks on IDF positions in northern Israel in an 18-hour period, the highest volume since the conflict began. Israel prepares for a major expansion of its Lebanon ground offensive.
Military U.S. Aircraft Loss. A U.S. Air Force KC-135 Stratotanker refueling aircraft comes under attack and crashes in western Iraq, marking one of the first confirmed U.S. aircraft losses of the conflict.

During Operation Rising Lion (June 2025) — a surgical, limited airstrike campaign targeting nuclear facilities — equity markets shrugged off the shock and closed the period modestly positive, peaking at +2.36% on Day 5. Operation Epic Fury tells a different story: the combination of leadership decapitation, Strait of Hormuz closure, QatarEnergy force majeure, and oil breaching $100/bbl has pushed the S&P 500 to a −3.54% drawdown by Day 9. The trajectory continues to diverge with no ceasefire in sight. The original Rising Lion conflict ultimately resolved without full-scale escalation; Epic Fury has not.

Analyst Note The oil market's response to Epic Fury is categorically different from Rising Lion. During Rising Lion, WTI gained a modest 7–8% over the conflict window as markets assessed supply risk from Iranian nuclear strikes without Hormuz disruption. Epic Fury's oil shock is of a different magnitude: a 42% WTI surge in nine trading days driven by Hormuz closure, QatarEnergy force majeure, and direct strikes on Iranian oil infrastructure. The Brent–WTI spread remains elevated at ~$4.90/bbl (vs. ~$2.80 during Rising Lion), reflecting the market's continued pricing of seaborne supply disruption. The question now is whether sustained $100+ oil forces demand destruction or triggers a strategic petroleum reserve release — either of which could cap the upside.

Analyst Note The Strait of Hormuz is the world\'s most critical oil chokepoint — ~20 million barrels per day, roughly 20% of global supply, transit through it daily. The Epic Fury closure has exposed the hard limits of mitigation: existing pipelines (Saudi EPC, UAE Habshan-Fujairah) handle ~6M bbl/d, IEA emergency releases contribute ~5M bbl/d sustained at record pace, and shadow fleet rerouting adds ~1M bbl/d. This leaves an unmitigated residual gap of ~8M bbl/d that markets must absorb through price — explaining the sustained oil shock above $100/bbl.
